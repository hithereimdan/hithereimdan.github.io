Hello! I am a third-year computer science graduate student, advised by Brian Scassellati.

Research

I make computational models of personality.

My aim is to engender a rich human-robot experience. There can be no doubt that characters engage us; from talking cartoon mice to the people portrayed on your favorite television show, we watch and connect with characters -- ones whose personalities and stories we enjoy. I attempt to build robotic characters that keep people engaged.

Education and therapy. to Desperate Housewives, The personality of a character is a major factor in engaging people. As we build robots that people are meant to interact with, we stand to benefit from getting people more interested in working with a robot. I'm interested, specifically, in the potential contributions of a robot's model of emotion and/or personality to producing richer human-robot interaction. I use what psychology has illuminated about human nature as a starting point to my work, to equip a robot with believable emotional responses. The long term vision of this line of research is to better engage patients in socially-assistive robotic therapies.
