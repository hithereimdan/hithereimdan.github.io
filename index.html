Research

I build models of individual learning differences in order to build robot tutors that personalize their instruction to suit the particular needs of individual students. This is a difficult problem to solve, even if a content expert defines the skills that the robot is assessing and teaching in advance, because the robot must decode the many-to-many relationship between the observations it makes of the student and the internal skills those observations represent. For instance, a calculus tutor who observes a student incorrectly solve an integral equation faces the non-trivial task of assessing which skills the student may need to review. It is a non-trivial task because more than one skill can be necessary for a single step in a calculus problem and each step a student takes may reflect proficiency in one of many skills.
